3D Printing: Providing Motivation for STEM Achievement

Indicators: S5.TF, S5.A/E, S5.E

My interest in 3D printing began as a personal inquiry; however, I have grown to see that 3D printing has far greater potential within the field of education than I previously had considered. The attached research paper explores the utilization of 3D printing technologies within multiple contexts and explores the possibility that 3D printing technologies could potentially solicit enough interest and motivation to not only attract students to STEM studies but also help them excel within their STEM studies.

This research was conducted following APA guidelines and utilizes research on the effects of motivation on STEM success and correlates the level of motivation that 3D printing has shown to solicit from learners to deduce that 3D printing may be a viable option for attracting new learners to STEM areas of study (S5.TF, S5.A/E, S5.E).
