UALR 'Trojan Challenge' Mobile Orientation Application

Indicators: S1.A/E, S4.E, S4.CP, S4.L

The goal of the UALR ‘Trojan Challenge’ mobile orientation application was to provide a tool to assist new students and staff if becoming familiar with the UALR campus and community. A need was identified that showed that many of the university’s offices have to allocate much of their limited resources to assisting students with general questions and that there was a huge employee knowledge gap beyond the employee’s immediate department’s responsibilities. The application aims to be an easily accessible resource for students and new university staff to obtain the correct information for whatever questions they may have from a up-to-date, centrally managed resource.

The creation of the app was completed by a small team of my peers, led by me because of my present role within the UALR Office of Admissions and my personal knowledge and understanding of the identified need at hand (S4.CP, S4.L). We decided the need warranted a mobile application because of the prevalence of tablet computers and smartphones among UALR’s student body and staff (S1.A/E). To ensure that our application was accessible and effective for the diverse student and staff population at the university, our team made sure that our application was void of any specific references to race, sexuality or gender. Our goal was to create a simple app that could be utilized and effective for all possible users (S4.E).
