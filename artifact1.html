Learning Systems Technology Orientation

Indicators: S1.C, S3.C, S3.A/E, S3.M, S3.DoL

The attached project is a completed instructional design created to facilitate new online students’ transition to a solely online degree program and to the UALR graduate school. The orientation’s design focuses on introducing learners to pertinent information for successfully managing their learning through Blackboard, addressing graduate school decorum, and how to organize and plan their schedule for the LSTE program from the offset.

The design was created utilizing the ADDIE process and incorporates multiple learning principles and best practices to ensure that the learning environment maintains effectiveness and proper alignment with the established objectives (S1.C, S3.C). The design was created to accommodate a diverse learner population because of its intended use within a public Graduate school (S3.DoL). To achieve this, multiple forms of assessment are utilized to ensure that the curriculum is accessible to all members of the learner population. The design is further made accessible through the use of formative and summative evaluations that allow for the gathering of data during implementation that could be used to improve the design (S3.A/E, S3.M). This is essential for informing changes and understanding how the instruction could be improved to accommodate a diverse learner population.
